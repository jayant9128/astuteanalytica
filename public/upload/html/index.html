Global Tungsten Carbide (WC) Based Thermal Spray Coating Market is estimated to witness a rise in revenue from US$ 708.4 Mn in 2021 to US$ 1,436.8 Mn by 2031. The market is registering a CAGR of 7.3% during the forecast period 2022-2031. 

Thermal spraying is a general term for a coating method in which a consumable is applied as a spray of finely separated molten or semi-molten droplets. When Tungsten Carbide Coating is applied to a substrate, it forms an instant bond with the surface, resulting in high bond strength and excellent wear resistance. Thus, have high adoption in various end use applications including automotive, aerospace & aviation, power generation, chemicals & petrochemicals, electronics, metals & metallurgy, oil & gas, healthcare & medical devices and others.

Market Dynamics

The global tungsten carbide (WC) based thermal spray coating is attributed to drive over the forecast period due to the high penetration of tungsten carbide (WC) based thermal spray coating in various end use application. Factors such as rising demand for high-velocity oxy-fuel (HVOF) tungsten carbide spraying across various industries, surging demand for tungsten carbide (WC)-based thermal spray coating in the mining and oil & gas and tungsten carbide-based coatings as alternatives to electrodeposited liard chromium are responsible for driving the market’s growth. Moreover, the advancement in technology such as HVAF spraying, and its positive features as compared to HVOF creates a lucrative growth opportunity for the market over the forecast period. However, high cost of HVOF tungsten carbide coating based thermal spray coating may hamper the market’s growth.

Impact of Regulation

According to the U. S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Federal Facilities Restoration and Reuse Office (FFRRO), provides a summary for tungsten, including physical and chemical properties, environmental and health impacts, existing federal and state guidelines, detection and treatment methods and additional sources of information. Moreover, they also provide basic information on tungsten to site managers and other field personnel who may address tungsten contamination at cleanup sites. Tungsten was originally considered a stable metal in soil that does not dissolve easily in water. However, tungsten-contaminated environmental media are now a growing concern to the EPA and the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) because recent research indicates that tungsten may not be as stable as was indicated in earlier studies.

Impact of COVID-19 on Global Tungsten Carbide (WC) Based Thermal Spray Coating Market 

The COVID-19 pandemic outbreak had a negative impact on the global tungsten carbide (WC) based thermal spray coating market. Many project schedules were changed or postponed due to lockdown and stringent controls implemented by governments. Industrial activities and plant construction projects across all industries were also hindered as a result of these interruptions. Moreover, due to decreased demand and supply chain disruptions, in the near term at least, the global HVOF tungsten carbide coating market will witness stunted growth.

Tungsten carbide is a chemical carbide made up of tungsten and carbon atoms in equal amounts and is roughly twice as strong as steel. 

Tungsten carbide is used to make not just parts, but also to coat stainless steel and other metal alloys with specific carbide coatings to improve strength.

HVAF, HVOF and PTA among others are the various available technologies for the thermal spray coating. 

HVAF tungsten carbide coatings are generally superior to HVOF regarding wear/erosion/cavitation resistance, corrosion protection and production cost.
